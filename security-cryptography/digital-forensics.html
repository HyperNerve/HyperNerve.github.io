Digital Forensics

Digital Forensics is the process of identifying, preserving, analyzing, and presenting digital evidence from electronic devices. It plays a critical role in investigating cybercrimes, data breaches, and legal disputes involving digital data.

1. Objectives

  • Recover and analyze digital evidence
  • Support legal investigations and incident response
  • Maintain integrity and chain of custody

2. Types of Digital Forensics

  • Computer Forensics: Analysis of desktops, laptops, and servers
  • Mobile Forensics: Extraction from smartphones and tablets
  • Network Forensics: Investigation of traffic and logs
  • Cloud Forensics: Evidence from cloud platforms
  • Malware Forensics: Dissection of malicious software

3. Example: Investigating a Compromised Laptop

Device: Windows laptop

Steps: Create disk image → Recover deleted files → Analyze browser history → Extract login credentials

Outcome: Identified unauthorized access and data exfiltration

4. Forensic Process

  1. Identification: Locate potential evidence sources
  2. Preservation: Secure and image data without alteration
  3. Analysis: Examine files, logs, and metadata
  4. Documentation: Record findings and procedures
  5. Presentation: Prepare reports for legal or organizational use

5. Tools & Software

  • Autopsy / Sleuth Kit
  • FTK (Forensic Toolkit)
  • EnCase
  • Volatility (memory analysis)
  • Wireshark (network capture)

6. Legal Considerations

  • Maintain chain of custody
  • Ensure evidence admissibility in court
  • Follow jurisdictional laws and privacy regulations

7. Best Practices

  • Use write blockers during acquisition
  • Document every step of the investigation
  • Verify findings with multiple tools

8. Challenges

  • Encrypted or deleted data
  • Rapidly evolving technologies
  • Cloud and remote evidence collection

9. Applications

  • Cybercrime investigations
  • Corporate breach analysis
  • Legal evidence recovery
  • Incident response and reporting
