Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Artificial Intelligence refers to the simulation of human intelligence in machines that are programmed to think, learn, and make decisions. It encompasses a wide range of techniques, from rule-based systems to deep learning.

1. Types of AI

  • Narrow AI: Performs specific tasks (e.g., spam filtering, facial recognition)
  • General AI: Hypothetical AI with human-level reasoning across domains
  • Superintelligent AI: Surpasses human intelligence (theoretical)

2. Core Disciplines

  • Machine Learning: Algorithms that learn from data
  • Natural Language Processing (NLP): Understanding and generating human language
  • Computer Vision: Interpreting visual data
  • Robotics: Physical agents that perceive and act
  • Expert Systems: Rule-based decision-making

3. AI vs. Machine Learning vs. Deep Learning

ConceptDescriptionExample
AIBroad field of intelligent systemsChess-playing bot

Deep LearningNeural networks with many layersImage recognition

4. AI Techniques

  • Rule-Based Systems: IF-THEN logic
  • Search Algorithms: A*, Minimax
  • Reinforcement Learning: Learn via rewards (e.g., Q-learning)
  • Neural Networks: Layers of interconnected nodes

5. Example: Rule-Based Expert System

IF temperature > 38°C AND cough THEN diagnosis = "Possible Flu"

6. Example: Neural Network

Input: Handwritten digit image
Layers: Input → Hidden → Output
Output: Digit classification (0–9)

7. AI Applications

  • Healthcare: Diagnosis, drug discovery
  • Finance: Algorithmic trading, fraud detection
  • Transportation: Autonomous vehicles
  • Customer Service: Chatbots, virtual assistants
  • Manufacturing: Predictive maintenance

8. Tools & Frameworks

  • TensorFlow: Deep learning library
  • PyTorch: Flexible neural network toolkit
  • OpenCV: Computer vision library
  • spaCy: Industrial-strength NLP

9. Challenges in AI

  • Data Dependency: Requires large, clean datasets
  • Bias & Fairness: Risk of discriminatory outcomes
  • Explainability: Black-box models are hard to interpret
  • Security: Vulnerable to adversarial attacks

10. Ethical Considerations

  • Privacy: Respect user data
  • Accountability: Who is responsible for AI decisions?
  • Transparency: Make AI behavior understandable
  • Autonomy: Avoid over-reliance on machines
