Computational Linguistics

Computational Linguistics is the scientific study of language from a computational perspective. It combines linguistics, computer science, and artificial intelligence to model, analyze, and process human language.

1. Core Concepts

  • Syntax: Structure and grammar of sentences
  • Semantics: Meaning of words and phrases
  • Phonetics & Phonology: Sound patterns and pronunciation
  • Pragmatics: Contextual meaning and usage

2. Key Techniques

  • Tokenization and part-of-speech tagging
  • Parsing (constituency and dependency trees)
  • Named Entity Recognition (NER)
  • Sentiment analysis and text classification
  • Machine translation and transliteration

3. Tools & Libraries

  • NLTK (Natural Language Toolkit)
  • spaCy (industrial-strength NLP)
  • Stanford CoreNLP
  • OpenNLP and TextBlob
  • UDPipe and Universal Dependencies

4. Linguistic Resources

  • WordNet (lexical database)
  • Treebanks (annotated syntactic corpora)
  • PropBank and FrameNet (semantic role labeling)
  • Parallel corpora for translation models

5. Applications

  • Speech recognition and synthesis
  • Chatbots and conversational agents
  • Information retrieval and question answering
  • Language modeling and text generation
  • Cross-lingual information access

6. Programming & Modeling

  • Python for NLP pipelines
  • Regular expressions for pattern matching
  • Hidden Markov Models and CRFs
  • Transformer-based models (BERT, GPT, T5)

7. Challenges

  • Ambiguity and polysemy in natural language
  • Low-resource languages and dialects
  • Bias and fairness in language models
  • Multimodal and code-switched language processing

8. Future Directions

  • Zero-shot and few-shot language understanding
  • Multilingual and cross-cultural NLP
  • Neuro-symbolic language models
  • Real-time translation and summarization
