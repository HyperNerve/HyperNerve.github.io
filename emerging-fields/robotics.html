Robotics

Robotics is the interdisciplinary field focused on the design, construction, operation, and application of robots. It integrates mechanical engineering, electronics, computer science, and AI.

1. Core Components

  • Sensors: Perception of environment (e.g., cameras, LIDAR, IMU)
  • Actuators: Motors and servos for movement
  • Controllers: Embedded systems or microcontrollers
  • Software: Algorithms for control, navigation, and decision-making

2. Types of Robots

  • Industrial robots (assembly lines)
  • Service robots (cleaning, delivery)
  • Medical robots (surgery, rehabilitation)
  • Humanoid robots (social interaction)
  • Swarm robots (collective behavior)

3. Key Technologies

  • ROS (Robot Operating System)
  • SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping)
  • Path planning algorithms (A*, D*, RRT)
  • PID and adaptive control systems

4. Programming & Simulation

  • Python and C++ for control logic
  • Gazebo and Webots for simulation
  • Arduino and Raspberry Pi for prototyping
  • OpenCV for computer vision

5. Applications

  • Manufacturing automation
  • Search and rescue operations
  • Space exploration (rovers, drones)
  • Military and defense systems
  • Human-robot interaction

6. Challenges

  • Real-time decision making
  • Robustness in dynamic environments
  • Energy efficiency and battery life
  • Safety and ethical concerns

7. Future Directions

  • Soft robotics and bio-inspired designs
  • Collaborative robots (cobots)
  • AI-driven autonomous systems
  • Robots with emotional intelligence
